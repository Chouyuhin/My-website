## Arrival in the United States

I arrived in the United States for the first time in September 2025, beginning my research fellowship at Harvard University. The journey was filled with anticipation and excitement about starting this new chapter of my academic career.

## First Impressions of Harvard Yard

Harvard Yard, the historic heart of Harvard University, immediately struck me with its timeless beauty and academic atmosphere. The centuries-old brick buildings, lush green lawns, and bustling student life created a captivating environment.

### Notable Landmarks

- **John Harvard Statue**: The iconic statue that everyone touches for good luck.
- **Widener Library**: An impressive neoclassical building housing millions of volumes.
- **Memorial Church**: A beautiful Georgian-style church at the center of the Yard.
- **Massachusetts Hall**: The oldest standing building at Harvard, dating back to 1720.

![Harvard Yard on my first day](/images/harvardfirstday.jpg "First day at Harvard Yard")

## Academic Significance

Walking through Harvard Yard, I felt deeply connected to the long tradition of scholarship and innovation that has shaped this institution. From its founding in 1636 to its current status as a global leader in education and research, Harvard represents the pursuit of knowledge at the highest level.

## Personal Reflections

This visit marked not just a physical arrival but the beginning of an important academic journey. As a research fellow, I look forward to contributing to the vibrant intellectual community while drawing inspiration from the rich history surrounding me.

## Looking Ahead

The Harvard Yard tour served as an inspiring introduction to my time at Harvard. It reinforced my commitment to academic excellence and reminded me of the incredible opportunities that lie ahead in my research work.
